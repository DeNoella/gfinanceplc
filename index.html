02 â€” Main Threats â€” Ibitero Bikunze Kubaho

The Most Common Threats
You Will Face

Hackers don't always break in through computers. Very often, they trick people. Here are the four main dangers you need to know about.

Important: Most cyberattacks succeed because of human error â€” not technical failures. A single click on a suspicious link can compromise our entire bank. Your awareness is our most powerful security tool.

Ingenzi: Ibitero byinshi byo kuri murandasi bifata kubera icyaha cy'umuntu â€” si ubusembwa bw'ikoranabuhanga. Ukanda rimwe kuri link iteye amakenga bishobora kugira ingaruka ku banki yacu yose. Ubumenyi bwawe ni icyuma cyacu cy'umutekano gikomeye cyane.

01

Phishing Emails

A fake email that looks real, pretending to be your boss, IT, or a vendor. It tricks you into clicking a bad link or sharing your password. When in doubt, don't click!

Kinyarwanda

Ubutumwa bwa email bw'ibinyoma busa nk'ubw'ukuri, bwitwaza ko buvuye ku muyobozi wawe, ku ishami rya IT, cyangwa ku mucuruzi mufatanya. Bugushuka gukanda link mbi cyangwa gutanga ijambo ry'ibanga (password) ryawe. Iyo ushidikanya, ntukande!

02

Ransomware

A virus that locks all your computer files and demands money to unlock them. It can shut down the entire branch! It often arrives through email attachments or USB drives.

Kinyarwanda

Ni virusi ifunga dosiye zose ziri muri mudasobwa yawe igasaba amafaranga kugira ngo zifungurwe. Ishobora no guhagarika ibikorwa by'ishami ryose! Akenshi iza binyuze mu ma attachment ya email cyangwa USB.

03

Impersonation / Pretexting

Someone pretends to be a manager, vendor, or IT staff to get you to transfer money or share information. They may sound very convincing â€” always verify independently!

Kinyarwanda â€” Kwiyitirira undi

Umuntu yiyitirira umuyobozi, umucuruzi, cyangwa umukozi wa IT kugira ngo agusabe kohereza amafaranga cyangwa gutanga amakuru. Bashobora kumvikana neza cyane â€” buri gihe banza ugenzure mbere yo gukora icyo basabye!

04

USB Drives (Baiting)

A USB drive left in the printer or desk is labeled something tempting like "Salaries 2025." If you plug it in, your computer gets infected. Never plug in unknown USB drives!

Kinyarwanda â€” USB Zitajyanye N'akazi

USB ishyizwe ku icapiro cyangwa ku meza, yanditseho amagambo ashukisha nka "Imishahara 2025." Iyo uyinjije muri mudasobwa, ihita iyanduza virusi. Ntuzigere winjiza USB utazi aho yaturutse!

04 Real-Life Scenario Urugero rwo mu Buzima Busanzwe

The Urgent Email That
Could Cost Millions

English Case Study

The Urgent Email That Could Cost Millions

Amina works in the Finance department. One morning, she receives an email that looks exactly like it's from the CFO. It says: "Amina, please urgently change the vendor bank details in the system and approve the transfer. I'm in a meeting. Do not delay."

The email looks perfect right logo, right signature. But something feels off. Amina decides to call the CFO directly using his number from the company directory (not from the email). The CFO confirms: he sent no such email.

Amina reports it to IT Security immediately. The attackers had created a fake email address almost identical to the real one. A similar scam in Kenya resulted in a loss of over KSh 1.5 billion.

Lesson: Always verify unusual or urgent requests by calling back on a known number. Urgency is a manipulation tactic. When in doubt stop, verify, report.
